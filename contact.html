Detailed Assam Solar Subsidy Guide

PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana is creating awareness across India, including Assam. Households in Guwahati, Goalpara, Barpeta, Nagaon, Dibrugarh, Silchar and other districts are exploring rooftop solar for savings and energy independence.

Who Can Apply in Assam?

  • Residential households with active electricity connection
  • Homes with usable rooftop area
  • Applicants with proper identity and bank documents
  • Consumers meeting technical feasibility checks

Electricity Board / DISCOM Notes

Applicants should keep local electricity bill details ready. Consumer number, meter details and billing records should match the application.

Best System Size for Assam Homes

  • 1kW - Low usage households
  • 2kW - Small family homes
  • 3kW - Medium family homes
  • 5kW+ - Higher monthly consumption

Expected Benefits

A properly sized rooftop solar system may significantly reduce daytime electricity dependence and monthly bills over time.

Documents Needed

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Latest Electricity Bill
  • Bank Passbook
  • Address / Ownership proof if required

Common Mistakes Assam Applicants Should Avoid

  • Wrong consumer number
  • Unreadable bill upload
  • Name mismatch in documents
  • Choosing wrong system size
  • Ignoring roof shade issues

Goalpara Special Note

In towns like Goalpara, rooftop solar can be especially useful for households seeking lower recurring electricity costs. Roof direction, shade from nearby trees and available area should be checked before installation.

Guwahati & Urban Homes

Urban users often benefit from higher electricity usage patterns, making solar ROI more attractive when rooftop access is available.

Smart Tip for Assam Users

Check your last 6 months electricity bills before applying. This helps choose a realistic solar capacity instead of overpaying for unnecessary size.
