Detailed Eligibility Guide 2026

PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana is designed to increase rooftop solar adoption among Indian households. While many families may qualify, final approval usually depends on technical feasibility, documentation and local DISCOM process.

1. Residential Households

This scheme generally targets homes rather than factories, offices or commercial shops. If the electricity connection is domestic, your chances are usually stronger.

2. Rooftop Suitability

Solar panels need sunlight exposure, structural safety and adequate space. Small roofs may still support smaller systems such as 1kW or 2kW.

3. Meter & Billing Status

Pending dues, incorrect consumer data or inactive connections may create delays. Keep your bill updated and connection active.

4. Joint Ownership Cases

If property is jointly owned, consent or additional documents may be needed depending on process requirements.

5. Tenant / Rented House Cases

Tenants may face approval challenges because rooftop rights and ownership matter. Owner permission may be necessary.

Common Reasons for Rejection

  • Name mismatch in documents
  • Wrong consumer number
  • No rooftop feasibility
  • Unreadable uploads
  • Unapproved installer selection
  • Ownership dispute

Best Way to Improve Approval Chances

  • Use correct Aadhaar details
  • Use latest electricity bill
  • Choose suitable system size
  • Keep bank details accurate
  • Use clear document scans

For Assam Applicants

If applying from Assam, keep local DISCOM details ready and ensure consumer number matches your bill exactly.
